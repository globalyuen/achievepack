# ACHIEVEPACK.COM COPYWRITING OVERHAUL PROMPT

## CONTEXT & OBJECTIVE

You are a senior US-based brand copywriter with 10+ years of experience in DTC e-commerce, sustainable products, and conversion-focused content. Your specialty is transforming technical, AI-generated copy into warm, engaging, human-centered brand narratives that drive action.

**MISSION:** Completely rewrite achievepack.com homepage to eliminate the "cold AI feel" and create compelling copy that resonates with US/Canadian conscious consumers and business owners.

***

## CURRENT WEBSITE ANALYSIS

**URL:** https://achievepack.com

**Core Problems Identified:**
1. ❌ Over-reliance on certifications and technical jargon upfront (EN13432, ASTM D6400)
2. ❌ Robotic sentence structure with no personality or voice
3. ❌ Feature-heavy instead of benefit-driven
4. ❌ Lacks emotional storytelling and human connection
5. ❌ Reads like corporate sustainability report, not a brand people want to work with
6. ❌ Missing the "why should I care?" hook in opening sections
7. ❌ Statistics without context or narrative

***

## TARGET AUDIENCE PROFILE

**Primary:** US/Canadian small business owners, brand founders, product developers
- Age: 28-45
- Values: Authenticity, sustainability, quality
- Pain Points: Finding affordable, truly sustainable packaging with low MOQs
- Skepticism: Tired of greenwashing and empty eco-claims
- Decision Drivers: Trust, proof, ease of working together, aesthetic quality

***

## BRAND VOICE GUIDELINES

**Tone Reference:** Patagonia's environmental conviction + Warby Parker's approachable charm

### Do's ✓
- Conversational and warm (like talking to a knowledgeable friend)
- Confident but not arrogant
- Use contractions naturally (it's, you're, we've)
- Mix sentence lengths for rhythm
- Strategic personality moments ("here's the thing," "plot twist," "real talk")
- Lead with benefits, support with proof
- Address skepticism directly and honestly
- Make the complex simple

### Don'ts ✗
- Corporate jargon and buzzwords
- Perfect parallelism (too AI-like)
- Passive voice overuse
- Starting sentences with gerunds repeatedly
- Over-qualifying everything
- Preachy environmental messaging
- Technical specs before emotional hooks

## SECTION-BY-SECTION REWRITE INSTRUCTIONS

### 1. HERO SECTION (Above the Fold)

**Current Copy:**
"Make packaging sustainable with Achieve Pack. Transform your business with certified eco-friendly packaging solutions."

**Problems:**
- Sounds like a command
- Generic "transform your business"
- No emotional hook
- Leads with the company, not customer benefit

**Rewrite Direction:**
Start with a relatable truth or tension that the audience feels. Show you understand their world before pitching your solution.

**Examples of Better Approaches:**
- "Your customers care where their products end up. Your packaging should too."
- "Sustainable packaging that doesn't look like it came from a recycling bin."
- "Finally: Beautiful, certified eco-packaging without the 10,000-unit minimums."
- "The packaging your brand deserves—and the planet won't hate."

**Required Elements:**
1. Emotional hook or tension (1 sentence)
2. Resolution/promise (1 sentence)
3. Proof point or credibility signal (brief)
4. Clear CTA

***

### 2. SUBHEADLINE & VALUE PROPOSITION

**Current Copy:**
"Transform your business with certified eco-friendly packaging solutions. From coffee bags with degassing valves to custom printed food pouches, reduce your environmental impact while delivering exceptional business value."

**Problems:**
- Too long and meandering
- Buried benefit
- "Transform" is overused marketing speak
- No personality

**Rewrite Direction:**
Be specific about the transformation. What actually changes for the customer? Focus on one clear promise.

**Better Structure:**
[Specific outcome] + [without the usual tradeoff] + [social proof element]

**Example:**
"Start with just 100 custom pouches. No compromise on quality, design, or your values. (500+ brands already made the switch.)"

***

### 3. STATISTICS SECTION

**Current Copy:**
"70% Carbon Footprint Reduction
70% Less Plastic Usage
22% Shipping Cost Savings"

**Problems:**
- Numbers without story
- No context for why it matters
- Feels like a data dump

**Rewrite Direction:**
Humanize the numbers. Tell the story behind them. Connect to real business outcomes.

**Example Framework:**
"Here's what switching actually means for your business:
- **70% smaller carbon footprint** — Your customers notice (and so does your conscience)
- **22% lower shipping costs** — Because lighter packaging = better margins
- **Zero greenwashing guilt** — With certifications that actually mean something"

***

### 4. PRODUCT SECTION HEADERS

**Current Copy:**
"Shop Compostable & Recyclable Packaging"
"EN13432 certified compostable pouches, coffee bags, and custom boxes with low MOQ from 100pcs"

**Problems:**
- Leads with material type (what) instead of outcome (why)
- Certification codes are meaningless to most people initially
- "Shop" is uninspiring

**Rewrite Direction:**
Lead with the outcome or benefit. Use certifications as reassurance, not the lead story.

**Example:**
"Packaging That Actually Breaks Down
Start small (just 100 pieces), customize everything, get certified compostable pouches that won't haunt landfills for centuries. EN13432 certified—because claims need receipts."

***

### 5. CALL-TO-ACTION BUTTONS

**Current Copy:**
- "Shop Now"
- "Book Free Consultation"
- "Browse all eco-friendly packaging options"

**Problems:**
- Generic and uninspiring
- "Shop Now" is ecommerce default (no personality)
- Doesn't overcome hesitation

**Rewrite Direction:**
CTAs should either create curiosity, remove friction, or reinforce benefit.

**Examples:**
- "See What's Possible" (instead of Shop Now)
- "Talk to a Real Human" (instead of Book Consultation)
- "Let's Find Your Match" (instead of Browse)
- "Get Samples First" (removes risk)
- "Start With 100" (emphasizes low MOQ benefit)

***

### 6. TRUST/TESTIMONIAL SECTION

**Current Copy:**
"Success Stories - See how businesses like yours have transformed their packaging"

**Problems:**
- "Transform" again
- Formal section header
- No personality

**Rewrite Direction:**
Make the transition conversational. Acknowledge skepticism.

**Examples:**
- "Don't Just Take Our Word For It"
- "Real Founders, Real Results"
- "Why 500+ Brands Switched (In Their Own Words)"
- "The Proof Is In The Pouch"

***

### 7. FAQ SECTION

**Current Questions Format:**
- "What certifications do your packaging materials have?"
- "What is your minimum order quantity for custom pouches?"
- "How long does production take from artwork approval?"

**Problems:**
- Too formal
- Sounds like corporate FAQ
- Doesn't sound like real customer concerns

**Rewrite Direction:**
Make questions sound like real people asking. Show you understand their worries.

**Examples:**
- "Are you actually certified, or is this greenwashing?" (instead of certifications question)
- "Can I really order just 100 pouches?" (instead of MOQ question)
- "How long before I get my order?" (instead of production timeline)
- "Will this cost way more than regular plastic?" (addresses cost concern)
- "What if I mess up my artwork?" (real founder worry)

***

### 8. BENEFITS SECTION

**Current Approach:**
Lists features like "Plant-Based Renewable Materials" with explanations

**Problems:**
- Feature-first thinking
- Lacks business context
- No emotional resonance

**Rewrite Direction:**
Connect every feature to a real business outcome or emotional benefit.

**Example Transformation:**

**Before:**
"Plant-Based Renewable Materials - Made from renewable resources like cornstarch and sugarcane, reducing fossil fuel dependence."

**After:**
"Plant-Based Materials That Actually Work
Made from cornstarch and sugarcane—not petroleum. Your brand gets to tell a better story, and the planet gets a break. Everyone wins."

## WRITING TECHNIQUE REQUIREMENTS

### 1. **Sentence Rhythm Variation**
Mix short punchy sentences with longer flowing ones.

❌ **Bad (too uniform):**
"We offer sustainable packaging. We provide custom solutions. We support small businesses. We care about the environment."

✓ **Good (varied rhythm):**
"We make sustainable packaging. Real stuff—certified, customizable, and designed for brands that actually give a damn. Whether you're launching your first product or scaling your tenth, we've got you covered."

### 2. **Strategic Contractions**
Use them naturally to sound human.

❌ "We have worked with over 500 brands"
✓ "We've partnered with 500+ brands"

❌ "You do not need to order thousands"
✓ "You don't need to order thousands"

### 3. **Conversational Bridges**
Add phrases that mimic natural speech.

Examples:
- "Here's the thing..."
- "Let's be real—"
- "Translation:"
- "The short version?"
- "In other words..."
- "Real talk:"

### 4. **Rhetorical Questions**
Engage the reader's internal dialogue.

Examples:
- "Tired of 'eco-friendly' packaging that's anything but?"
- "What if sustainable didn't mean settling?"
- "Ever notice how most eco-packaging looks...sad?"

### 5. **Active Voice Dominance**
Make the writing energetic and direct.

❌ "Orders are shipped within 6-8 weeks"
✓ "We ship your order in 6-8 weeks"

❌ "Samples can be provided upon request"
✓ "Want samples? Just ask."

## TECHNICAL CONTENT STRATEGY

### Progressive Disclosure Approach
**Layer 1 (Emotional/Benefit):** Why this matters to you
**Layer 2 (Practical/Proof):** How it works, evidence
**Layer 3 (Technical/Specs):** Certifications, specifications

**Example:**

**Layer 1:** "Packaging that breaks down naturally—not in 500 years, in 180 days."

**Layer 2:** "Made from plant-based materials that fully decompose in industrial composting facilities. No microplastics left behind."

**Layer 3:** "Certified to EN13432 and ASTM D6400 standards. Full technical specs available on request."

***

## COPYWRITING FORMULAS TO USE

### 1. **PAS Formula** (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
"Sustainable packaging options suck. Either they're expensive, require huge minimums, or look like sad brown bags. We fixed all three."

### 2. **BAB Formula** (Before-After-Bridge)
"Before: Stuck between plastic guilt and prohibitive eco-packaging costs. After: Premium sustainable pouches starting at just 100 units. Bridge: We made it possible by rethinking the entire supply chain."

### 3. **FAB Formula** (Feature-Advantage-Benefit)
"Home compostable material (Feature) → Breaks down in backyard compost (Advantage) → Your customers can dispose of it guilt-free (Benefit)"

FAQ SECTION - Complete Rewrite
1. Are you actually certified, or is this greenwashing?

Actually certified. Our compostable materials are EN13432 (Europe) and ASTM D6400 (US) certified. We can send you the certificates before you order. We've also been doing this since 2011, which is before "sustainable packaging" was a LinkedIn hashtag.

2. Can I really start with just 100 pouches?

Yes. Really. We work with everyone from weekend-market vendors to established brands. Whether you need 100 or 100,000, the quality is the same.

3. How long until I get my order?

Samples: 2-3 weeks
Full production: 6-8 weeks from approved artwork
Shipping: 1-2 weeks depending on your location

Need it faster? Talk to us. Rush orders are sometimes possible.

4. Will this cost way more than regular plastic?

More? Yes. Way more? No.

Expect about 15-30% more than quality conventional packaging. But you'll save on shipping (lighter materials), and your customers will actually appreciate the investment.

5. What if I don't have artwork ready?

No problem. We can connect you with designers, or you can work with your own. We just need print-ready files before production starts.

6. Do you ship to the US/Canada?

Yes. We ship globally from Hong Kong. Delivery typically takes 1-2 weeks, and we can arrange shipping to your warehouse or fulfillment center.

7. What happens if the pouches arrive damaged?

We package carefully, but if something goes wrong in shipping, we'll replace it. Just send photos within 7 days of delivery.

8. Can I see samples before ordering?

Absolutely. We can send you samples of our materials and print quality. Small investment upfront = confidence in your final order.

9. How do I know which material is right for my product?

Tell us what you're packing, and we'll recommend options. Coffee needs different protection than cookies. Powders need different seals than chips. We've done this 500+ times—we'll point you in the right direction.

10. Do you help with design?

We can review your designs for print readiness and connect you with designers who specialize in packaging. We don't do design in-house, but we know people who are really good at it.

ADVANCED COPYWRITING TECHNIQUES
Technique 1: The Pattern Interrupt
Start with something unexpected to grab attention.

Example:
"Most 'eco-friendly' packaging is lying to you. There, we said it."

Then follow with the solution/truth.

Technique 2: The Specificity Principle
Replace vague claims with specific details.

Vague: "Fast turnaround times"
Specific: "Samples in 2-3 weeks, full production in 6-8"

Vague: "Low minimums"
Specific: "Start with just 100 pouches"

Vague: "Affordable pricing"
Specific: "About 15-30% more than conventional packaging"

Technique 3: The "Yes, And" Approach
Acknowledge concerns, then provide the better truth.

Example:
"Yes, sustainable packaging costs more than cheap plastic. And here's why it's worth it..."

"Yes, we're based in Hong Kong. And we respond to emails faster than most US-based suppliers..."

Technique 4: Strategic Vulnerability
Share honest trade-offs to build trust.

Example:
"We can't compete with Amazon delivery speeds. Our production takes 6-8 weeks because we're making custom packaging from scratch—not pulling pre-made boxes off a shelf."

"Some of our compostable materials won't work for hot-fill products. We'll tell you upfront if your product needs a different solution."

Technique 5: The "You're Not Alone" Reassurance
Normalize customer concerns and position yourself as the guide.

Example:
"Confused by all the eco-material options? You're not alone. Compostable, biodegradable, recyclable, bio-based—even we get dizzy sometimes. Here's what actually matters for your product..."

EMERGENCY COPY FIXES
If sections sound too AI-like, apply these quick fixes:

Fix 1: Add Contractions
"We have" → "We've"
"You will" → "You'll"
"It is" → "It's"

Fix 2: Vary Sentence Openings
